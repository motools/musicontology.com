Getting started

The Music Ontology provides a model for publishing structured music-related data on your web site or through your API. If you're unsure about why you should publish music-related data in this way, please read our FAQ. Below are a few typical examples of using the Music Ontology, in three different syntaxes: RDFa in HTML for embedding music-related data on a web page, and JSON-LD and Turtle for exposing music-related data as part of an API.

URIs

Before publishing Music Ontology data, the first thing to do is to think about the resources (e.g. tracks, artists, musical works) you want to describe and what URIs they should have. The main idea behind Linked Data revolves around identifying resources with "cool URIs" so that other people can link to them and add information about them. Those URIs should be persistent and as unambiguous as possible (i.e. identifying only one thing, so that other people can link to the right one).

One of the easiest way of managing URIs is to use fragment identifiers, or "hash URIs" and we will be using such URIs in the examples below.

There are also large sets of URIs available for the music domain, such as those provided Musicbrainz. It is often easier to reuse those URIs rather than creating new ones. Doing so also means that your data is linked with other music-related data, therefore making it richer.

Adding prefixes

In order to start using the Music Ontology, you will need to add a few prefixes to your document. This is done by adding the following to your document.

In HTML:

<html prefix="mo: http://purl.org/ontology/mo/
              dc: http://purl.org/dc/elements/1.1/
              xsd: http://www.w3.org/2001/XMLSchema#
              tl: http://purl.org/NET/c4dm/timeline.owl#
              event: http://purl.org/NET/c4dm/event.owl#
              foaf: http://xmlns.com/foaf/0.1/">
  ...

<html>

In JSON-LD:

{
  "@context": {
    "mo": "http://purl.org/ontology/mo/",
    "dc": "http://purl.org/dc/elements/1.1/",
    "xsd": "http://www.w3.org/2001/XMLSchema#",
    "tl": "http://purl.org/NET/c4dm/timeline.owl#",
    "event": "http://purl.org/NET/c4dm/event.owl#",
    "foaf": "http://xmlns.com/foaf/0.1/"
  }
  ...
}

In RDF/Turtle:

@prefix mo: <http://purl.org/ontology/mo/> .
@prefix dc: <http://purl.org/dc/elements/1.1/> .
@prefix xsd: <http://www.w3.org/2001/XMLSchema#> .
@prefix tl: <http://purl.org/NET/c4dm/timeline.owl#> .
@prefix event: <http://purl.org/NET/c4dm/event.owl#> .
@prefix foaf: <http://xmlns.com/foaf/0.1/> .
...

The main reason for these multiple prefixes is that the Music Ontology doesn't redefine terms that are defined in other vocabularies. For example the Music Ontology is very commonly used alongside FOAF, a vocabulary for describing people and including terms such as 'name', 'homepage', 'member', etc. RDF is designed to mix and match terms from multiple vocabularies, and the Music Ontology makes a heavy use of that.

Describing a track

The following example shows how to describe a track, the band who made it, and linking the band to its MusicBrainz identifier. It uses the Music Ontology alongside FOAF and Dublin Core.

In HTML:

<span typeof="mo:Track" about="#track-1">
  <span property="dc:title">Turnover</span>
  is a track by 
  <a rel="foaf:maker" typeof="mo:MusicGroup" 
     href="http://musicbrainz.org/artist/233fc3f3-6de2-465c-985e-e721dbabbace#_">
    <span property="foaf:name">Fugazi</span>
  </a>
</span>

In JSON-LD:

{
  "@id": "http://example.org/#track1",
  "@type": "mo:Track",
  "dc:title": "Turnover",
  "foaf:maker": {
    "@id": "http://musicbrainz.org/artist/233fc3f3-6de2-465c-985e-e721dbabbace#_",
    "@type": "mo:MusicGroup",
    "foaf:name": "Fugazi"
  }
}

In RDF/Turtle

<#track-1>
  a mo:Track ;
  dc:title "Turnover" ;
  foaf:maker <http://musicbrainz.org/artist/233fc3f3-6de2-465c-985e-e721dbabbace#_> .
<http://musicbrainz.org/artist/233fc3f3-6de2-465c-985e-e721dbabbace>
  a mo:MusicGroup ;
  foaf:name "Fugazi" .

Describing a band

The following example shows how to describe a band and its members.

In HTML:

        
<span typeof="mo:MusicGroup" about="#band-1">
  <span property="dc:title">Fugazi</span>
  started in <span property="mo:activity_start" value="1987-01-01T00:00:00Z">1987</span>
  and has the following members:
  <ul rel="foaf:member">  
    <li about="#imk" typeof="mo:MusicArtist"><span property="foaf:name">Ian MacKaye</span></li>
    <li about="#jl" typeof="mo:MusicArtist"><span property="foaf:name">Joe Lally</span></li>
    <li about="#gp" typeof="mo:MusicArtist"><span property="foaf:name">Guy Picciotto</span></li>
    <li about="#bc" typeof="mo:MusicArtist"><span property="foaf:name">Brendan Canty</span></li>
  </ul>
</span>

In JSON-LD:

{
  "@id": "http://example.org/#band-1",
  "@type": "mo:MusicGroup",
  "dc:title": "Fugazi",
  "mo:activity_start": "1987-01-01T00:00:00Z",
  "foaf:member": [{
    "@id": "http://example.org/#imk",
    "@type": "mo:MusicArtist",
    "foaf:name": "Fugazi"
  },{
    "@id": "http://example.org/#jl",
    "@type": "mo:MusicArtist",
    "foaf:name": "Joe Lally"
  },{
    "@id": "http://example.org/#gp",
    "@type": "mo:MusicArtist",
    "foaf:name": "Guy Picciotto"
  },{
    "@id": "http://example.org/#bc",
    "@type": "mo:MusicArtist",
    "foaf:name": "Brendan Canty"
  }]
}

In RDF/Turtle:

<#band-1>
   rdf:type mo:MusicGroup;
   dc:title "Fugazi";
   foaf:member <#imk>;
   foaf:member <#jl>;
   foaf:member <#gp>;
   foaf:member <#bc> .
<#imk>
   rdf:type mo:MusicArtist;
   dc:title "Ian MacKaye" .
<#jl>
   rdf:type mo:MusicArtist;
   dc:title "Joe Lally" .
<#gp>
   rdf:type mo:MusicArtist;
   dc:title "Guy Picciotto" .
<#bc>
   rdf:type mo:MusicArtist;
   dc:title "Brendan Canty" .

The following example shows how to associate the band members with a period of time and an instrument.

In HTML:

<span prefix="ical: http://www.w3.org/2002/12/cal/ical#" typeof="mo:MusicGroup" about="#band-1">
  <span property="dc:title">Fugazi</span>
  has the following members:
  <ul rel="mo:membership">
    <li typeof="mo:Membership">
      <span rel="mo:artist"><span about="#bc" typeof="mo:MusicArtist" property="foaf:name">Brendan Canty</span></span> played
      <a rel="mo:primary_instrument" typeof="mo:Instrument" href="http://dbpedia.org/resource/Drum_kit">drums</a>
      from <span property="ical:dtstart" value="1987-01-01T00:00:00Z">1987</span>
    </li>
  </ul>
</span>

In JSON-LD:

{
  "@id": "http://example.org/#band-1",
  "@type": "mo:MusicGroup",
  "dc:title": "Fugazi",
  "mo:membership": [{
    "@type": "mo:MusicMembership",
    "ical:dtstart": "1987-01-01T00:00:00Z",
    "mo:artist": {
      "@type": "mo:MusicArtist",
      "foaf:name": "Brendan Canty"
    },
    "mo:primary_instrument": {
      "@type": "mo:Instrument",
      "@id": "http://dbpedia.org/resource/Drum_kit"
    }
  }]
}

In RDF/Turtle:

<#band-1>
   rdf:type mo:MusicGroup;
   dc:title "Fugazi";
   mo:membership [
     rdf:type mo:Membership;
     mo:artist <#bc>;
    mo:primary_instrument <http://dbpedia.org/resource/Drum_kit>;
   <http://www.w3.org/2002/12/cal/ical#dtstart> "1987-01-01T00:00:00Z"^^xsd:dateTime .
<#bc>
   rdf:type mo:MusicArtist;
   foaf:name "Brendan Canty" .
<http://dbpedia.org/resource/Drum_kit>
   rdf:type mo:Instrument .

The music production workflow

The Music Ontology model for music production is based on a chain of events, from the composition of a given musical work to a recording of one of its performances. This enables the ontology to be very descriptive and easily extensible. Below is an illustration of such a workflow.

We describe below Bach's "The Art of Fugue" and one of its performances.

In RDF/Turtle:

<http://dbpedia.org/resource/Johann_Sebastian_Bach>
    rdf:type mo:MusicArtist ;
    foaf:name "Johann Sebastian Bach" .
<#composition>
    a mo:Composition ;
    dc:title "Composition of The Art of Fugue, BWV 1080: Contrapunctus IX, a 4, alla Duodecima" ;
    mo:composer <http://dbpedia.org/resource/Johann_Sebastian_Bach> ;
    event:time [ tl:start "1742"^^xsd:gYear ; tl:end "1749"^^xsd:gYear ] ;
    mo:produced_work <#kunst-der-fuge> .
<#kunst-der-fuge>
    a mo:MusicalWork ;
    dc:title "The Art of Fugue, BWV 1080: Contrapunctus IX, a 4, alla Duodecima" .
<#performance>
    a mo:Performance ;
    mo:performance_of <#kunst-der-fuge> ;
    event:time [ tl:at "2003"^^xsd:gYear ] ;
    mo:performer <http://musicbrainz.org/artist/fd20e8ed-0736-44db-8d97-530fbf00e813#_> ;
    mo:instrument [ rdfs:label "Violin" ], [ rdfs:label "Violin" ], [ rdfs:label "Viola" ]; [ rdfs:label "Cello" ] ;
    mo:recorded_as <http://musicbrainz.org/recording/93da082b-99c1-4469-9eb6-91e9fbeaeb2a#_> .
